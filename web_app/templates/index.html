Analysis of multispectral thermal data from ISRO VEDAS and Bhuvan portals indicates severe heat trapping in commercial and high-density residential wards across Delhi NCR, with surface temperatures exceeding 46.0°C during peak summer hours. Without immediate intervention, urban sprawl is projected to add +2.4°C over the next 15 years.

2. High-Priority Wards Immediate Outlay

Immediate capital deployment is recommended for Chandni Chowk (W106), Connaught Place (W101), and Karol Bagh (W102). These zones exhibit >85% concrete imperviousness coupled with critical population density defined by WorldPop India 100m grids.
